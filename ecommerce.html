Borderlands Mexico: Toyota bets big on Texas, moves Tacoma line to San Antonio

Toyota is shifting Tacoma pickup production from Baja California to San Antonio as part of a $3.6 billion expansion that will double its Texas manufacturing campus and create 2,000 jobs, with the transition completing by 2030. The move signals automakers' broader recalibration of North American manufacturing amid USMCA uncertainty and nearshoring trends, even as cross-border freight demand is projected to grow from $91.1 billion in 2025 to $119.4 billion by 2031.

How much is trade policy influencing imports?

Inbound ocean container bookings into the US are holding near annual highs as tariff uncertainty persists, with the Section 122 surcharge set to expire July 24, 2026, and the administration pivoting to Sections 301 and 232 for future tariff authority after courts struck down previous measures. The booking pattern suggests shippers frontloaded orders ahead of potential duty increases, but the bulge of orders extending 2-4 weeks out indicates restocking and consumer demand strength are driving imports as much as tariff avoidance.
